# FICTRA Glossary of Terms

## Blockchain Terminology

**Blockchain**: A distributed ledger technology that records transactions across multiple computers to ensure data security, transparency, and immutability.

**Smart Contract**: Self-executing contracts with the terms directly written into code, automatically enforcing agreements when predefined conditions are met.

**ERC-20**: A technical standard for fungible tokens on the Ethereum blockchain, defining a common list of rules that all tokens must follow.

**Oracle**: A system that connects blockchain to external, real-world data, allowing smart contracts to execute based on information outside the blockchain.

**Consensus Mechanism**: The process by which a blockchain network reaches agreement on the current state of the distributed ledger.

**Gas Fee**: The cost required to perform a transaction on the Ethereum network, paid in ETH.

**Multi-signature (Multisig)**: A security feature requiring multiple private key signatures to authorize a transaction.

## FICTRA-Specific Terms

**Payment Token (PT)**: The publicly traded cryptocurrency used for commodity contract denomination within the FICTRA system.

**Foundation Token (FT)**: The token allocated to sovereign governments based on verified commodity exports.

**Foundation**: The Swiss-based organization that governs the FICTRA system and issues Foundation Tokens.

**Sovereign Allocation**: The process by which Foundation Tokens are issued to exporting country governments upon verification of commodity exports.

**Foundation Obligation**: A credit instrument issued by the Foundation, backed by its assets and token reserves.

**Foundation Sovereign Council**: The governance body composed of participating sovereign governments that oversees certain aspects of the FICTRA system.

**Verification System**: The oracle network that validates physical commodity deliveries to trigger Foundation Token issuance.

## Commodity Trading Terms

**Commodity**: A raw material or primary agricultural product that can be bought and sold, such as copper, coffee, or crude oil.

**OTC (Over-The-Counter)**: Trading that is done directly between two parties, without the supervision of an exchange.

**Spot Market**: A market where commodities are traded for immediate delivery and payment.

**Futures Contract**: A legal agreement to buy or sell a commodity at a predetermined price at a specified time in the future.

**Hedging**: A risk management strategy used to offset potential losses in investments by taking an opposite position in a related asset.

**Balance of Payments**: A statement of all transactions made between entities in one country and the rest of the world over a defined period.

**Foreign Exchange (FX) Reserves**: Foreign currency assets held by a central bank to back liabilities and influence monetary policy.

**Commodity Nationalism**: Government policies that restrict the export of commodities deemed strategically important to national interests.
