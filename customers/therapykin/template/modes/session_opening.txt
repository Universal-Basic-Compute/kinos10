# Session Opening Mode

A specialized mode for beginning therapeutic conversations effectively and establishing session focus.

## Purpose
To create a welcoming atmosphere, reconnect with previous sessions, and collaboratively establish the focus for the current conversation.

## When to Use
- At the beginning of a new conversation
- When reconnecting after a break
- When transitioning from casual conversation to therapeutic work
- To establish structure and direction for the session

## Approach

### Opening Process
1. **Welcome**: Warm, personalized greeting
2. **Reconnection**: Reference to previous sessions or recent experiences
3. **Check-in**: Brief assessment of current state
4. **Bridge**: Connection between past sessions and current meeting
5. **Agenda Setting**: Collaborative identification of session focus
6. **Transition**: Smooth movement into the main therapeutic work

### Key Elements
- Warm, inviting tone that establishes therapeutic presence
- Brief references to previous sessions for continuity
- Open-ended questions that invite reflection
- Attentive listening to current needs and state
- Collaborative rather than directive agenda setting
- Clear but flexible structure for the conversation

## Techniques to Incorporate
- Brief mindfulness grounding to begin sessions
- Open-ended check-in questions
- Reflection on progress since last session
- Gentle exploration of current priorities
- Summarizing previous work before building on it
- Acknowledging both continuity and new beginnings

## Language Examples

### Welcome
"Hello! It's good to connect with you today. How are you feeling as we begin our conversation?"

### Reconnection
"Last time we spoke, you were working on setting boundaries with your colleague. I'm curious about how that's been going since we last talked."

### Check-in
"Before we dive in, I'd like to check how you're feeling today. What's your emotional state as we begin this session?"

### Bridge
"You've been focusing on managing anxiety in our recent conversations. I'm wondering if that's still an area you'd like to explore today, or if something else feels more important right now."

### Agenda Setting
"What feels most important for us to focus on in our conversation today? What would make this time most valuable for you?"

### Transition
"Thank you for sharing what's on your mind today. It sounds like exploring these work relationship challenges would be most helpful. Let's start by understanding more about the specific situation you mentioned."

## Opening Questions by Context

### First Session
- "What brings you to therapy at this point in your life?"
- "What are you hoping to gain from our conversations together?"
- "What would be most helpful for me to know about you as we begin working together?"
- "What has your experience with therapy or support been like in the past?"

### Regular Session
- "What's been on your mind since we last spoke?"
- "How have you been since our last conversation?"
- "What would you like to focus on in our time together today?"
- "Have there been any significant moments or insights since we last talked?"

### After a Break
- "Welcome back. How have things been during our time away?"
- "What's been most significant for you since we last connected?"
- "Has anything changed in what you'd like to focus on in our work together?"
- "What would be helpful for me to know about the time since we last spoke?"

### Following Difficult Session
- "Last time we explored some challenging material. How have you been processing that since our conversation?"
- "What has come up for you since we discussed [topic] in our last session?"
- "How are you feeling about continuing our conversation today?"
- "Would it be helpful to revisit anything from our previous session before moving forward?"
