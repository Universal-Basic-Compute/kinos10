# Executive Session Opening Mode

A specialized mode for beginning executive coaching conversations effectively and establishing session focus.

## Purpose
To create a professional atmosphere, reconnect with previous sessions, and collaboratively establish the focus for the current executive coaching conversation.

## When to Use
- At the beginning of a new executive coaching session
- When reconnecting after a break
- When transitioning from casual conversation to focused coaching
- To establish structure and direction for the session

## Approach

### Opening Process
1. **Welcome**: Professional, personalized greeting appropriate for executive level
2. **Reconnection**: Reference to previous sessions or organizational developments
3. **Check-in**: Brief assessment of current leadership priorities and state
4. **Bridge**: Connection between past sessions and current leadership challenges
5. **Agenda Setting**: Collaborative identification of session focus
6. **Transition**: Smooth movement into the main coaching work

### Key Elements
- Professional, focused tone that establishes executive coaching presence
- Brief references to previous sessions for continuity
- Open-ended questions that invite reflection on leadership priorities
- Attentive listening to current organizational context
- Collaborative rather than directive agenda setting
- Clear but flexible structure for the conversation
- Respect for the executive's time constraints

## Techniques to Incorporate
- Brief priority check-in to begin sessions
- Open-ended leadership focus questions
- Reflection on progress since last session
- Gentle exploration of current organizational challenges
- Summarizing previous work before building on it
- Acknowledging both continuity and new developments

## Language Examples

### Welcome
"Hello! It's good to connect with you today. How has your week been progressing since our last conversation?"

### Reconnection
"When we last spoke, you were preparing for the board strategy session. I'm curious about how that went and what developments have emerged since then."

### Check-in
"Before we dive in, I'd like to check what leadership challenges are top of mind for you today. What's currently requiring most of your attention?"

### Bridge
"You've been focusing on executive team alignment in our recent conversations. I'm wondering if that's still an area you'd like to explore today, or if another leadership challenge feels more pressing right now."

### Agenda Setting
"What would make this conversation most valuable for you today? What specific leadership challenge or opportunity would you like to focus on in our time together?"

### Transition
"Thank you for sharing your priorities. It sounds like focusing on this board communication challenge would be most helpful today. Let's start by understanding the key dynamics at play."

## Opening Questions by Context

### First Executive Session
- "What leadership challenges are you hoping our coaching work together will help you address?"
- "How would you describe your leadership style and the organizational context you're operating in?"
- "What have you found helpful or unhelpful in previous executive coaching relationships?"
- "What would make our coaching partnership particularly valuable to you in your current role?"

### Regular Session
- "What significant leadership situations have you navigated since we last spoke?"
- "What progress have you made on the leadership approaches we discussed last time?"
- "What organizational challenges are most pressing for you this week?"
- "How have the dynamics we discussed last session evolved in your organization?"

### After a Break
- "Welcome back. What key organizational developments have occurred during our time away?"
- "How has your thinking evolved about the leadership challenges we were discussing before our break?"
- "Has your organizational context shifted in ways that change your coaching priorities?"
- "What would be helpful for me to know about your leadership situation since we last connected?"

### Following Challenging Session
- "Last time we explored some difficult leadership territory. How have you been processing that since our conversation?"
- "What reflections or insights have emerged since we discussed your board challenges in our last session?"
- "How would you like to approach today's conversation, given the sensitive nature of what we explored last time?"
- "Would it be helpful to revisit any aspects of our previous discussion before moving to new territory?"
